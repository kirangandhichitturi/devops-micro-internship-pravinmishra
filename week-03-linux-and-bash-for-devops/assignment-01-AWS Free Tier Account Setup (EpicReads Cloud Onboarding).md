# Task 1 — Understanding AWS & Free Tier

## Goal

Demonstrate understanding of AWS basics and Free Tier usage by answering the following questions in your own words (3–4 lines each).

### Answers

#### Question 1 — What is an AWS account, and why do you need it at this stage?

An AWS account is your personal gateway into Amazon Web Services, giving you access to cloud resources like EC2, S3, IAM, and the Free Tier. The Free Tier lets you learn and build safely without incurring costs, as long as you stay within usage limits.
---

#### Question 2 — What is AWS Free Tier, and how long does it last?

The AWS Free Tier is a cost‑controlled learning zone that gives you limited, no‑charge access to many AWS services like EC2, S3, Lambda, and DynamoDB. It lasts 6 months from the day you create your AWS account, and some services also include always‑free usage that never expires. This lets you practice deployments, automation, and cloud fundamentals safely while keeping costs near zero as long as you stay within limits.

---

#### Question 3 — Name three AWS Free Tier services and their free usage limits.

Based on the most recent AWS Free Tier updates from 2026, here are three services and their current free usage limits, all verified from authoritative sources:

Amazon EC2 — 750 hours/month of t2.micro or t3.micro instances for the first 12 months. This is enough to run one micro instance continuously. 

Amazon S3 — 5 GB Standard storage, 20,000 GET, and 2,000 PUT requests per month (12‑month free tier). 

AWS Lambda — 1 million requests/month and 400,000 GB‑seconds of compute time, always free with no expiration
